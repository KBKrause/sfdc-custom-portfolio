Who am I?


My name's Kevin. I'm a freshly minted software developer.


I've dabbled a little bit in everything - web development, desktop apps, APIs, DevOps, and even the cloud.

Down the road, I see myself becoming some type of software architect.


I really enjoy learning about and working with enterprise applications like Salesforce, SAP, Oracle, AWS and Heroku.


I will openly admit that I'm not the best developer, but I'm very resourceful and ambitious.


I think knowing HOW to get the answer is better than knowing WHAT the answer is.


Right now, I do technical consulting work revolving around the Salesforce platform. I've specialized in Mulesoft ESB and integrations. I have 7 Salesforce certifications and 1 Mulesoft certification.


Thanks for checking out my website. Please browse around and reach out if you'd like to know more!

Some of my favorite projects . . .


"Chemberry"  Description of icon when needed



I created Chemberry out of my love for chemistry and code.
It is a microcontroller programmed to facilitate note taking and experiment analysis in laboratories.
Programmed in Java and C/C++, the Raspberry Pi is the GUI while the Arduino collects data using various lab instruments.

"AMBER14 MD Analyzer"  Description of icon when needed



AMBER "is a suite of biomolecular simulation programs."
It produces large datasets that are unwieldy and troublesome to analyze by hand.
In college, we used this program to help analyze some of the datasets produced by our experiments.
We published two papers in the Journal of Dispersion Science using some of these results.
