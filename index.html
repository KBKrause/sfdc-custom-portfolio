Who are you?


My name's Kevin. I'm a budding software developer. I prefer back-end stuff, but I enjoy tinkering with front-end as well.


I'm a big picture person and enjoy solving big picture problems (read: not getting lost in a complicated regex expression. But I will if I HAVE to).


I really enjoy learning about and working with enterprise architecture like Salesforce, SAP, Oracle, and cloud computing platforms like AWS and Heroku. I'm getting into DevOps as well.


I will openly admit that I'm not the best developer around, but I'm very resourceful and ambitious. I think knowing HOW to get the answer is better than knowing WHAT the answer is.


Right now, I do technical consulting work revolving around the Salesforce platform. I've specialized in Mulesoft ESB and integrations. I have 7 certifications.


Thanks for checking out my website. Please browse around and reach out if you'd like to know more!

Some of my favorite projects . . .


"Chemberry"



I created Chemberry out of my love for chemistry and code.
It is a microcontroller programmed to facilitate note taking and experiment analysis in laboratories.
Programmed in Java and C/C++, the Raspberry Pi is the GUI while the Arduino collects data using various lab instruments.

"AMBER14 MD Analyzer"



AMBER "is a suite of biomolecular simulation programs."
It produces large datasets that are unwieldy and troublesome to analyze by hand.
In college, we used this program to help analyze some of the datasets produced by our experiments.
We published two papers in the Journal of Dispersion Science using some of these results.
